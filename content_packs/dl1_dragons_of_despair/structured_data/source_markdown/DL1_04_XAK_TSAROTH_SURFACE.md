# Xak Tsaroth — Surface Ruins (Areas 44a–44k)
## DL1: Dragons of Despair — Rules Engine Reference

When adventurers enter the ancient city, they begin from area 44a. Use the **Swamp Map**.

---

## AREA 44a: Swamp Ruins
Ironclaw tree roots twist into mud and swamp water. Vines, trees, and ferns block vision beyond a few feet. Ground soaked and spongy. All in shadow.

**Occasional ruins (1 in 6 per round):** Broken foundation stones, shattered urns, ancient carvings jut from underbrush.

**Tracks:** Many creatures passed through within last 3–4 hours. Faintly reptilian prints. Heroes who have met draconians: 20% chance to recognize them.

---

## AREA 44b: Waters of the Swamp
Green scum on dark swamp water. Sour, metallic smells.

**Water depth:** Unpredictable — 1d6 feet per 30 feet traveled.

**Whenever anyone enters the water for any reason:** Roll 1d6. If result < 4: roll 1d4+20 on the random encounter table (Appendix 4) to determine what creature rises up to attack.

---

## AREA 44c: Vine Bridges
Thick vines woven into strong ropes stretched between two sections of land. Each end knotted around several Ironclaw trees. Slimy mosses cover the ropes.

**Crossing:** Surprisingly sturdy. Check DEX at –5 to cross without trouble. If failed: PC falls into water below — triggers monster roll per area 44b rules.

---

## AREA 44d: Battle of the Fallen Ironclaw
Soaked ground slopes into thick swamp water. Giant Ironclaw tree has fallen, spanning the waters. Thick green moss drapes the log.

Behind trees and bushes, only 15–20 feet away: **6 Bozak Draconians.** Four on heroes' side, two on far side.

**Tactic:** Plan to capture party for questioning at camp (44f). Attack first with **web** spells (save at –2 or tangled in trees behind PCs). Then attempt to **sleep** party one at a time. Take captives to 44f.

**Retreat:** If they take more than 50% losses, they retreat.

**What they know:** Crystal staff is a very powerful item; stolen from the ruins; the one thing their leader Verminaard fears can upset his plans.

## AREA 44f: Temple of Baaz (Draconian Camp)
Massive ruins, small broken towers. Ferns and bushes through flagstone plaza with small shacks and lean-tos. Huge bonfire at north end. Beyond fire: crumbled dome with a **huge black dragon** figure with wings outfolded.

**Dragon is a wicker model.** However, draconians nearby attack any unescorted intruders.

**If PCs are prisoners:** Equipment placed randomly in one of six huts; PCs held in bamboo cage.

| Creature | AL | MV | HP | AC | HD | Attacks | Damage | Spells | Notes |
|----------|----|----|----|----|----|---------|----|--------|-------|
| Bozak Draconian Priest ×1 | LE | 6"[15"]/18" | 23 | 2 | 4 | 1 or 2 | 1–8 or 1–4/1–4 | 1st: charm, MM, shield; 2nd: invis, mirror image | Explodes on death |
| Baaz Draconians ×7 | LE | 6"[15"]/18" | 8×2,9,5,11,10,3 | 4 | 2 | 1 or 2 | 1–8 or 1–4/1–4 | — | Turn to stone, crumble |

**Priest behavior:** Runs the camp and conducts worship before the wicker dragon. Orders PCs held for 3 days until Khisanth (the priest's commander) comes to claim them. If party has the staff: "The crystal staff is the greatest danger to our masters we know." If no staff: asks where it is. Either way tells party: "Now that you are here, we only hope that you do not die as easily as those of Que-Shu did at our hands."

**The Wicker Dragon — Trick:**
- Opening at base between its legs; main wing braces tied together just below mouth; mouth forms a funnel
- If a PC gets inside unnoticed, leaps on main brace, and shouts through the mouth: panics draconians and drives them screaming into jungle for 1d6 turns
- **30% chance** the structure falls face-first into the bonfire and burns furiously
- Anyone inside must check DEX to escape; takes 1d6 damage each round DEX check is failed

**The Bamboo Cage:**
- 1 Baaz draconian guards it night and day
- 10% chance per daylight hour the guard falls asleep; 30% chance per hour after sundown
- Successful bend bars/lift gates check breaks the bars open

## AREA 44g: Tower of Truth
Graceful crumbling tower rises from swamp waters. Finely carved stonework still glints under undergrowth. Wooden floors and rafters have long rotted away. Carved staircase circles up interior to stone floor at top.

**View from top:** All surrounding swamplands visible. Cluster of ruins (44k) lies to the northeast.

---

## AREA 44h: Breeding Pool
Pool of black water amid towering jungle trees; not even mighty Ironclaws grow in this water. Five cracked leathery egg shells on shore (~2' long each). Dragon hatchlings lurk beneath water and rise to attack if PCs disturb the surface.

## AREA 44i: Swamp Falls
Soggy jungle floor ends suddenly in a **1,000-foot cliff**. Swamp water trickles down the stone bluff to a crashing seashore below. The Newsea spreads to the eastern horizon — covering what was once a fertile plain.

---

## AREA 44j: Fallen Obelisk
Tremendous obelisk beside its broken pedestal base. Spidery runes cover its surface. Obelisk bridges two sections of ground divided by swamp water.

**Reading the runes:** 15% base chance; add read languages score if applicable.

**Rune text:**
> *The Great City of Xak Tsaroth*
> *Whose beauty surrounds you*
> *Speaks to the good of its people*
> *And their generous deeds.*
> *The gods reward us*
> *In the grace of our home.*

---

## AREA 44k: Plaza of Death
Broad cobblestone street, fluted columns, relief carvings of sagging buildings. Large cobblestone courtyard with four tall free-standing columns. In front of pillars: a **huge well** plunging into the earth, vapors rising. To the north: a single building stands whole (Temple of Mishakal, area 46).

**The Well:** Dark and terrible stillness. Gazing into it: nothing but vast blackness. Rushing air, dead cold, swells from below.

**Dragon Arrival:** Suddenly Khisanth bursts from the well and unfolds tremendous leather wings. She casts **Darkness** covering the entire area.

**Khisanth's Surface Battle Tactics:**
1. First combat round: gains speed in air, circles for better attack position
2. PCs susceptible to dragon fear (see Monster Manual p. 30)
3. Attacks from the air while hovering; avoids swords and hand weapons
4. Bombards heroes with **magic missile** and **sleep** spells
5. Makes 2 aerial passes using 64-hp breath weapon (acid) each time
6. On 3rd pass: feels need for safety of lair; flies over well, folds wings, drops like stone down the well; breaks out of dive far below; moves toward lair

**After this encounter:** PCs may explore the well further (area 45). Heroes may also enter the building to the north (area 46a).
