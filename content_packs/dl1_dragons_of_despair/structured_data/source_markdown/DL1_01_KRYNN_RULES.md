# Krynn-Specific Rules
## DL1: Dragons of Despair — Rules Engine Reference

---

## Three Mandatory Campaign Differences

### 1. Currency — Gold Has No Value
Gold is too soft for swords or armor; it has no monetary value in Krynn. Steel is the primary valued metal.

**Experience:** 1 steel piece = 1 XP, regardless of where acquired.

**Seven trade metals:** steel, gold, silver, copper, iron, platinum, bronze.

**Seeker Lands currency (Emas system):**
| Coin | Abbreviation | Value |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Steel Emas (standard) | em | = 50 sp, or 10 cp, or 2 ip, or 2 pp, or 5 bp |
| Gold piece | gp | No monetary value in Seeker Lands |

**Dragonlord-occupied territory exchange:**
| Unit | Value |
|------|-------|
| 1 gpw of steel | = 10 gp, 20 sp, 100 cp, 2 ip, 1/5 pp, or 2 bp |

**Crossing into Dragonland:** 10 Seeker Emas (worth 500 sp in Seeker Lands) drops to 200 sp equivalent — a 60% value loss.

**Other abbreviations used in this module:**
- stl = steel piece; gpv = gold piece value (AD&D equivalent); gpw = gold piece weight; em = Emas

---

### 2. Clerical Magic — Lost for 300 Years
Clerical spells have not existed since the Cataclysm (~300 years ago). Characters who still call themselves clerics belong to worshipful orders of the Seekers but have **no spell abilities**.

**PC clerics from other campaigns:** Lose all spell-casting abilities immediately upon arrival in Krynn. No exceptions.

**Pseudo-clerics:** Use the standard cleric combat table but cast no spells.

**Adventure goal:** Recovering the Disks of Mishakal (area 70k) and learning to worship the true gods restores clerical spellcasting. Any cleric who worships the old gods of good (Paladine, Majere, Kiri-Jolith, Mishakal, Habakuk, Branchala) may receive spells per standard AD&D rules after reading the Disks.

---

### 3. Dragons — Believed Extinct (1,000+ Years)
No dragons have existed in Krynn for over 1,000 years. Most inhabitants:
- Smile when dragons are mentioned
- Believe dragons are folklore
- Almost nobody believes they currently exist

**DM note:** This means PCs from standard AD&D campaigns who know about dragons have knowledge that would mark them as eccentric or insane to locals.

## Kender (Halfling Equivalent)

Kender look like wizened 14-year-olds. Unlike halflings, **they wear shoes**. They have all standard halfling abilities PLUS:

### Special Ability 1: Taunt
Any creature the Kender taunts must **save vs. Spells** or attack wildly at once:
- Duration: 1–10 rounds
- Penalty to attacker: –2 to hit
- Penalty to attacker: +2 to AC (worse AC)

Kender are masters at verbal abuse and can enrage nearly any creature.

### Special Ability 2: Fearlessness
Kender are **completely immune to fear** — both magical and non-magical.

**Note:** Kender are, however, curious about everything; this tendency frequently gets them into trouble.

---

## Elves in Krynn

**All PC elves are Qualinesti Elves.** Other kinds of elves exist but appear in later DRAGONLANCE modules, not DL1. Do not allow other elf subraces for PCs in this module.

---

## Dragonlance Dragons — Fear Effect

When PCs encounter Khisanth (the dragon in this module), apply standard AD&D dragon fear per Monster Manual p. 30. This is referenced explicitly in area 44k.
