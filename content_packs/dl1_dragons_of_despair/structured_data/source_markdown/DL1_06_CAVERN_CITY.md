# Cavern City — Areas 53–70k
## DL1: Dragons of Despair — Rules Engine Reference

---

## AREAS 53–54: Upper Underground City

### 53a. Bakery
Sewer tube of fitted stone breaks through ceiling. Water trickles from tube onto floor and out the east door. 20 large rotting sacks on the floor south of the tube.

**If PC slides down the sewer into this room:** Launches into the rotting sacks — they disintegrate in an explosion of white. PC is covered in flour. No damage, but everyone they meet asks why they're covered in it until cleaned.

Windows in east wall overlook ruins of a city street. Sound of rushing water from that direction.

### 53b. Sidestreet
Toppled buildings leaning against each other form a rough arch of marble slabs over the cobblestone street. Still and tense, like the nave of a haunted cathedral. Doors and broken shop windows yawn open. No encounter.

---

### 54a. Sage Front
Books line the north wall, spines stained with green fungus. Rich, musty smell of rotting paper.

**Book titles:** Huma and the Lance of Dragons; Tales of the Greystone Wars; Chronicle of Kith-Kanan; The Gods of Krynn; Iconochronos, Vol. II (note for the DM: Astinus wrote the Iconochronos — the DM is the spirit of Astinus).

**Condition:** All books so rotted from swamp water they crumble at the touch.

### 54b. Sage's Court
Large golden chair sagging on a platform at the west end. Floating above the chair: the bright transparent form of a man in robes.

|----------|----|----|----|----|----|----|-------|

**Speech:** "I am Ossamis. It is my vow to answer one last question before I may leave. Ask: yours is the answer."

**What Ossamis knows:** The location of the **Disks of Mishakal (area 70k)** and how to get there.

## AREA 55: Treasury Court
Street emerges into a plaza. Tremendous cracked pillars under rock ceiling. Water from east and west streets flows into a large pool that empties into a river flowing west toward a steady roar. Across the plaza river: **blackened steel doors** stand in the face of a solid, square structure.

**Crossing the river (not at ford):** STR check (subtract 5 from roll) to cross. Kender and dwarves get no bonus. Fail: swept 10' further toward the falls (area 56). Must check again until success or going over the falls.

**Steel doors:** 1,500 gpw each. Worth 15,000 gp each in Solace, Haven, or Gateway. Counterpoised hinges; open easily.

## AREA 56: View from the Falls
Water rushing around columns and plunging from hanging steps into a vast cavern. Over **500 feet below**: ruins litter the dim cavern floor. Several waterfalls pour into the cavern; many streets are rivers, all flowing into a single abyss to the north. A **huge chain extends from the mists overhead down into a small courtyard below**.

**Vines to climb down:** Slippery and damp. Must pass **two DEX checks** to climb down without trouble. Fall from here: 20d6 damage + certainly attracts draconians.

**Gully Dwarves on the vines:** If first PC descends about 100 feet, they hear sniffling, muttering, and cursing — more Gully Dwarves climbing up to add their weight to the lifter.

## AREAS 57–59: Treasury Complex

### 57a. Outer Treasury
Long counter of chipped, dusty marble bisecting the room. Four ghostly forms at south wall with weapons drawn. Behind the counter: another long-faced ghostly form — the clerk.

| Guardian Spectral Minions ×4 | CE | 30" | 9,11,16,17 | 2 | 3 | 1 | 1–8 | 20% magic resist |

**Clerk demands:** Payment of **Kathal's taxes: 500 clay Tsarothian Culli**. Accepts no other currency. Payment releases him from duty.

**If PCs have no Culli:** Clerk orders guards to kill them for trying to impersonate Kathal.

**Counter:** 3' gap at east end; about 4' high. Spectral minions hover around it.

### 57b. Vault
Hundreds of rotting sacks, clay tokens spilling from split sides. Three skeletons (two holding shovels) sprawl half-buried in clay tokens. Two alcoves in east wall with moss-covered inscriptions.

**Reading inscriptions:** 30% chance. Northern one reads "Treasury"; southern one reads "Taxes."

**The alcoves:** Both are **permanent dimension doors** leading to the ruins of the Treasury Tower (area 62). They warp space to appear as normal arched entrances into the vault.

---

### 58a. North Cavern
Chimney fragments hang 5' from cavern ceiling. Water trickles from chimney and fissures. 15 feet below the chimney: cavern floor slants sharply toward the southwest.

### 58b. Drain
Steepening floor narrows into a smooth, yard-wide **vertical drain**. Water collected from all the cavern fissures swirls steadily down the sides of the shaft.

### 58c. Down the Sink
Water splashes down slick sides of the vertical shaft. Chilly; noise of the water is deafening.

---

### 59a. Windows Below
Natural shaft of smooth rock carries a spray of water into a cramped cave. Ceiling filled with water. Floor is made of solid masonry stonework — what appears to be a **30' tall window laid into the stone floor**. Glass is gone; ironwork of the window-frame remains.

### 59b. Dance on the Wall
A vast hall lying **on its side**: its northern wall (beamed like a ceiling) is one side; its southern wall (tiled floor) is another. Now the ceiling and floor of the room are windowed. Water pours in from upper windows, out through broken lower ones. Mosses and fungus everywhere. Fungus on the floor **appears to be moving**.

**The curtains:** From the northern end of each upper window (the former top), curtains of corroded woven metal hang down to the wall below. Support the weight of a man. Those from the westernmost window have been pulled over to an arched opening in the west wall, 30' up, fastened there — **this opening leads to a dark corridor**.

**Crossing from curtain to curtain:** Each adventurer must make a DEX check or fall to the floor (3d6 damage) and be attacked by the snakes.

## AREA 60: Entrance to the Hall of Justice
Only dim light from above scattering darkness onto ruins-covered cavern floor. A facade of an ancient building juts sideways from the cavern wall. Its roof lies half-buried in rubble to the north; steps now rising to the south. Delicately carved pillars lie broken amid rubble sloping west to a cobblestone street. Further west: ring of broken stones — foundation of a tremendous tower. The tower itself lies next to the foundation, its top buried in the cavern wall. Water pours into the cavern from several high places.

---

## AREA 61: North Cavern Falls
Water thunders down from an opening high up the cavern wall. Gleaming faintly, tumbles over rubble, forms a river on the cobblestone street, rushing out of sight.

---

## AREA 62: Treasury Tower
A broken foundation circles where a tremendous tower once stood. **Two 10' cubes** stand within the circle, apparently untouched by the catastrophe. Each cube has an arched opening in its side; the openings face each other, 20' apart. The fallen tower lies on its side, its top buried in the cavern wall to the north. Inside the hollow cylinder of the tower: only darkness.

**The cubes:** Permanent dimension doors, both leading to the vault (57b). Warp space so they appear to be normal arched entrances.

---

## AREA 63: North Mall
Catastrophe has marred once-beautiful buildings. Moss-covered rubble hides cobblestones. Icy river surges northwest; the street ends abruptly in a **chasm** — mist billows from the hole, water crashes somewhere below.

**Crossing the swift water (within 50' of the chasm):** Must check both STR and DEX to cross unaided. Fail either: swept 10' toward the twisting whirlpool in the crevasse. Must check again or be swept another 10' toward the maelstrom. Falling into the crevasse: drowns unless waterbreathing, in which case PC surfaces at the bottom of a **1,000' cliff** floating out into the Newsea.

## AREA 64: Palace Guard Hall
Shattered city walls. In middle of the street, a river flowing swiftly north. A shaft of dim light overhead centers on a great plaza to the south. One building holds up admirably. Dark growth obscures the lettering above its double doors.

**Reading the runes (60% chance):** "Guardians of Justice."

**The mural:** Heroes must clean it off before realizing it's a map of a city. Even discovered, only 30% chance of recognizing their current location on the map. Anyone who figures out which part is the cavern area can discover the way to the Great Plaza (67), the Hall of Justice (70a), and the Treasury Tower (62).

Dark stains. 20'-square room. Two sodden wooden doors open east and south. No encounter.

## AREA 69: The Secret Way

### 69a. Delvers Welcome
Water seeps down walls, pools, then winds out the south door. A long stone counter bisects the room east to west. Behind it: another doorway.

### 69b. The Secret Way
Rivulets of water trickle down the walls.

**Finding the trapdoor:** PCs who check the floor near the center of the room find a closed trapdoor operated by a pull ring. Add the STR of all PCs trying to pull the door open to find the **percentage chance of success**.

**The trapdoor:** 4'-square opening over a 4'-square shaft 20' deep. Opens into a 5'-diameter masonry tunnel half-filled with water.

**Before entering:** Check for a random encounter. If indicated, roll 1d20 for how many rounds later the encounter takes place. Treat a Khisanth encounter as **no encounter** (she's not down here).

**The tunnel layout:**
- North of the access shaft: runs 30' to a cave-in
- South from the shaft: runs 200' south, then turns east 340' to another cave-in
- **30 feet before the end of the east branching tunnel:** A shaft ascends 30' to a **grate in the floor of Khisanth's lair (70k)**

**If badly outnumbered:** Surrenders, then tries to lead heroes through the **west doors of area 70j** — insisting it's the only safe way to enter the dragon's lair. (It is not; it is a trap.)

### 70i. Long Hall
Water seeping down the arching walls of the 10'-wide hall. Cold, musty smell. Hall runs south into rubble of a collapsed ceiling. **Steel double doors (500 gpw each) stand closed on the right** — lead to area 70k.

### 70j. Hall of Sound
30'-high ceiling arches above 20'-wide hall. Mosaic patterns cover the floor. Hall is **90' long**.

**Pressure plates:** 40' from either end, pressure plates in the floor activate a **loud gong** that resounds through the palace area.

**If gong is triggered:** The dragon pushes her head out the east doors and uses her **breath weapon** on the party in this narrow space. She then calls for draconian guards (unaware there are none left in the area).

### 70k. Court of the Balance — DRAGON'S LAIR
A vast chamber, **100' in diameter**, 4 stories tall, cracked and broken translucent dome above. Dim light filters through mists above and spreads onto the center of the rotunda. There: a **glossy black dragon** straddles a pile of jewels, steel weapons, and intricate items.

**This is Khisanth's lair.** She is duty-bound to keep the Disks of Mishakal from the PCs.

## EPILOGUE

**If PCs break the staff, escape the cavern, and return to area 46b:**

The beautiful statue of the goddess towers above. In her hands lies a perfect carved likeness of the blue crystal staff. At the foot of the statue, a lone figure stirs.

**The lone figure:** The person who broke the staff. They lie here unharmed, all hit points restored. The staff is now part of the statue. A **platinum version of the Medallion of Faith** — bearing the symbol of the goddess — now adorns the neck of the PC who broke the staff.

**If PCs took the Disks without the Crystal Staff (didn't use it):** Allow them to make their way out of the cavern as best they can. Do not read the Epilogue box.

**Continuing to DL2:** If players continue with Dragons of Flame (DL2): the day ends as heroes trudge back over the Forsaken Mountains. Dark clouds flash in the west — but the billowing darkness is more than clouds. Smoke climbs into the autumn sky, rising from the **forests of Solace**.
