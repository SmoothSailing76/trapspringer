# Wilderness Encounters — Areas 1–43
## DL1: Dragons of Despair — Rules Engine Reference

Encounter areas are bordered by dotted lines on the wilderness map. Crossing a dotted border triggers the encounter at once. Think of them as large dungeon rooms. If PCs cross from one encounter area into one with the same number, simply repeat the encounter.

---

## AREA 1: Solace Township

Warm autumn Vallenwood treetown; ~500 people (not counting outlying farmers). No local government; ruled by Theocracy of Seekers from Haven (3 days west). Uses **Emas exchange system**. Gold bricks prop doors or hold down papers. Steel weapons carry a premium from smith Theros Ironfeld (most respected man in town).

All standard adventurer services available at reasonable rates. Exception: Theros charges extra for steel weapons.

Ancient stone bridge over Solace Stream. Road forks west/south. Concealed draconian ambush in trees, waiting for their leader (area 6) to sound a horn. PCs must climb a tree to spot them; even then only 30% chance.

## AREA 5: Prayer's Eye Peak
Flaming autumn colors; Prayer's Eye Peak to southwest. **30% chance** one party member (random) spots the White Stag. If enemies pursue heroes into this area, chance increases to **80%**.

| Creature | AL | MV | HP | AC | HD | Attacks | Damage |
|----------|----|----|----|----|----|---------|----|
| White Stag ×1 | LG | 24" | 77 | –5 | 10 | 3 | 1–12/1–6/1–6 |

**White Stag behavior:** Stays just ahead of heroes, leads them through the crack in Prayer's Eye Peak, then bounds at full speed into Darken Wood (area 24). Cannot be captured.

**If heroes kill the stag:** Dark thunderclouds form overhead; hover for 7 days; all players add +1 to their Armor Class (i.e., AC becomes worse by 1) during this time. Stag body disappears.

## AREA 7: Jakanth Vale
Unnatural stillness; heavily wrapped footprints lead to a campsite in a glade.

**Campsite:** Smells like burned hair. Charred bones in fire pit ashes. Grass stamped down.

**Treasure (searching):** Bright silver bracer with 4 gems (500 gpv each). Inside: "Firehawk, warrior of Que-Shu." Que-Shu Plainsmen identify this as the bracer of warriors charged with defending the tribe. These bands are forged around the warrior's arm — death alone removes them.

No combat encounter listed.

## AREA 17: Lordcity of Haven
White marble towers trimmed in gold. Low decorative wall with six towers. Inner circle 1 mile across; 3 miles including outer homes. Normal population ~5,000 (now overflowing). Nearly all standard adventurer services available; ironsmith works only for Highseekers.

**Key sub-areas:** 17a Steel Tankard Tavern, 17b Councilhall of Highseekers.

City overflows with refugees (2–20 present everywhere; HD 1–3, AC 8–10).

**Holy Guards on every street corner (10 per encounter):** AC 4, HD 4, MV 12", Dmg 1–8. Most pressing mission: find bearer of the blue crystal staff and bring them before the Council.

---

### 17a. Steel Tankard Tavern
Packed shoulder to shoulder. Three notable figures:
- **Man in fur cloak:** Doubts the Highseekers have any power; "Where is their magic? Where are their miracles?"
- **Forester:** Wonders why guards seek someone with a crystal staff; could it preserve them from invaders?
- **Woman with eye-patch:** All ways south are blocked. Qualinesti is closed. Darken Wood south entrance possible but no one returns. River White-rage between Darken Wood and Qualinesti is most dangerous. No happy choices.

---

### 17b. Councilhall of Highseekers
50'×30'×30' tall hall. Nine chairs in semicircle on polished granite floor. Nine white-robed men with steel girdle bands.

**Entry:** 100 Holy Guardians (CG, MV 12", AC 4, HD 4, Dmg 1–8) encircle building. Admit only those who bear news of the blue crystal staff.

| NPC | AL | MV | HP | AC | HD | Attacks | Damage |
|-----|----|----|----|----|----|---------|----|
| Master of Highseekers ×1 | CG | 12" | 35 | 8 | 7 | 1 | 1–8 |
| Highseeker Councilors ×8 | CG | 12" | 25,26,17,22,26,16,15,16 | 8 | 5 | 1 | 1–8 |

**Situation:** Draconian army to the north offered not to invade if staff is returned to Xak Tsaroth. Council's prayers to Newgods go unanswered; no standing army. Crystal staff is their last hope.

**If party has staff and reveals it:** Councilors beg to take it. If refused, they beg party to return it to Xak Tsaroth.

**Crystal staff's reaction:** Any councilor who touches the staff is hit by a lightning bolt for 4d6 damage. After this, Highseekers declare it an evil artifact and command party to return it to Xak Tsaroth.

*(Note: The Master is too dignified to rise and touch the staff — determine randomly which councilor tries, 1d8.)*

## AREA 18: River White-rage
Frothing powerful river formed by joining of several rivers. Easy crossing at the fords; impassable elsewhere without a raft.

**Swimming rules:** Any PC attempting to walk or swim across (non-ford): STR check to swim back to starting side, or be swept 100' downstream taking 1d12 drowning damage. Must check again until they make the check or drown. PCs cannot be swept to the other side.

**Raft building:** 1d4 hours per PC the raft is built to carry (6 PCs = 6d4 hours). River is safe for rafts; floating downstream enters area 20.

## AREA 19: Qualinesti Elflands
Thick aspen forest, trunks only a few feet apart. After more than 1 hour in area, 15 elves emerge and surround party.

| Creature | AL | MV | HP | AC | HD | Attacks | Damage |
|----------|----|----|----|----|----|---------|----|
| Elves ×15 | LG | 12" | 17 each | 5 | 2+2 | 1 | 2–9 (sword) or 1–6 (arrow) |

**Behavior:** Bows nocked, swords drawn; demand surrender. Order: take PCs to centaurs (area 23). They do not attack first.

**If party fights:** Additional elf army units, 1 per game hour (identical stats). Fight to the death for their homeland, cold fierceness. They do not pursue beyond north border of their land.

**Why they have these orders:** "A glorious being passed through our land and said you would be coming. We do not know his name, but we have orders to take you to the edge of Darken Wood at his bidding."

## AREA 20: The White-rage Cut
Tall cliffs funnel the river; crashing between two soaring bluffs.

**Raft navigation:** Check both STR and DEX once per turn. Fail either = raft splinters on rocks; all passengers fall into rapids. Raft ends up nearer north shore; use swimming rules from area 18. North shore survivors find paths up cliff face into area 22. Raft moves 2 miles per turn.

---

## AREA 21: Magic Mountains
Mountain valley between Prayer's Eye Peak and second ridge. Tall aspens, still green into autumn. Soft grass carpet. Exits to SW and E lead into deeper woods.

## AREA 22: Spirit Forest (Entry to Darken Wood)
Thickening canopy; dappled light fades to nothing; black knotted trees.

**Darken Wood confusion effect:** PCs who enter areas 22–26 are subject to its confusing charm. When leaving the Darken Wood, PCs **cannot express** what they saw or experienced there even though their memory is clear.

**Spectral Minion challenge:** PCs see strange shapes flitting through dense woods. Minions challenge them — ask their names.
- If they **answer falsely:** Spectral Minions attack
- If they **give their correct names OR show Crystal Staff:** Minions halt and let them pass

| Creature | AL | MV | HP | AC | HD | Attacks | Damage | Notes |
|----------|----|----|----|----|----|---------|----|-------|
| Spectral Minions ×12 | CE | 30" | 17×3,18×2,19×2,14×2,10×2,12 | 2 | 3 | 1 | 1–8 | 20% magic resist |

**If allowed to pass:** Leader says, "The Forestmaster awaits. You are expected." One spirit escorts them ~½ mile to area 26. Escort's backstory: "Ages ago, before the world changed, we were ordered to keep this land safe. That is the Purpose. We failed. Now we repent by protecting the land while we can."

## AREA 26: Unicorn Grove
Dense oak forest opens into a glade. Soft green grass up a hillside; rock outcropping at top. Forestmaster Unicorn stands atop the overhang, head raised high, strange sadness in its eyes.

| Creature | AL | MV | HP | AC | HD | Attacks | Damage |
|----------|----|----|----|----|----|---------|----|
| Forestmaster Unicorn ×1 | CG | 24" | 60 | 2 | 10 | 3 | 1–8/1–8/1–20 |

**Forestmaster's greeting:** "I am the Forestmaster. You have entered my Great Forest: I grant you passage and the aid of all creatures while you remain within our borders." From now until PCs leave the forest: no creature attacks unless PCs attack first.

**Forestmaster's message (from a great and glorious being who visited days ago):** "You must fly straight away across the Eastwall Mountains. Within two days you must be within Xak Tsaroth. There, if you prove worthy, you shall receive the greatest gift given to the world."

**The Forestmaster knows:** A terrible force of evil will shortly destroy the beauty of his domain.

**If PCs request help:** Forestmaster summons one Pegasus per PC. They circle silently overhead, land gently, allow mounting, then hurtle into the air and soar over the glade. PCs looking back see Forestmaster call after them.

**Upon leaving Darken Wood:** PCs subject to confusion of leaving (cannot express what they experienced). Must also make CON check each turn or fall asleep.

**If party attacks the Forestmaster:** He calls at once for magical forest creatures to defend him. Roll immediately on the Darken Wood Random Encounter table. From now on all creatures encountered attack on sight; if headed out of the woods, creatures only try to chase them out.

## AREAS 27–28: Gateway Pass / Gateway
**Area 27:** Gateway Pass — worn road peacefully between Sentinel Peak spires; brilliant leaves floating.

**Area 28:** Gateway town — comfortable houses and shops at base of Sentinel Mountains. Buildings on the ground (unlike Solace). No ironsmithy. Citizens know nothing of danger from demons or evil armies; dismiss such talk as tall tales — "Dragons in the night" is their name for stories of things that don't exist.

Burned stubble plains. Three roads converge at stone pillar pointing to Que-Teh, Que-Kiri, and Que-Shu. Cold wind; single hawk circles. Ground heavily trampled. Ranger (who has met draconians): 30% chance to identify draconian tracks. All draconian tracks lead east toward Que-Shu (area 36).

## AREA 36: Que-Shu
The entire Que-Shu tribe is gone. Village abandoned. Center square has a gruesome construction: two posts driven by terrible force, crosspiece 10' up, three chains, three charred and blackened non-human (hobgoblin) corpses suspended by feet. Sign on the crosspiece:

**Sign text (15% base chance to read; add read languages if applicable):**
*"Know ye, my servants, the fate of those who take prisoners against my will. Kill or be killed. Verminaard."*

Villager tracks lead east toward the mountains. Signs of rushed departure everywhere.
