## THE BLUE CRYSTAL STAFF (Crystal of Mishakal)

The central MacGuffin of the entire module. Carved from a single piece of blue crystal. About 5 feet long; 2-inch-diameter shaft. Ornamentally bladed head with a gem in its center.

### Origin
Although Riverwind does not remember, a manifestation of the Goddess Mishakal gave him the staff in the Forsaken Lands.

### Intelligence and Alignment
- **Intelligent artifact**
- **Alignment:** Lawful Good
- **Ego:** 10
- Can only be used by Lawful Good characters
- Non-LG characters who try to wield the staff: **take 4d6 points of electrical damage**
- An LG person may use the staff to heal the electrical damage it has caused to others

### Powers
**1. Striking:** Acts as a staff of striking (1–3 charges per use)

**2. Clerical spells** (2 charges per level of spell):
| Spell Level | Spells Available |
|-------------|-----------------|
| 1st | command, cure light wounds, remove fear |
| 3rd | continual light, cure blindness, cure disease, remove curse |
| 5th | cure critical wounds, raise dead |
| 7th | restoration, resurrection |

**3. Teleportation:** At its own discretion (using 15 charges)

**4. Dragon breath deflection:** In a 10' radius (10 charges per use)

### Charges
- Holds up to 20 charges at a time
- Regains 1 charge per day from the combined influence of Krynn's three moons
- Can be immediately and completely recharged at the statue of Mishakal (area 46b) by placing the staff in the statue's arms

### The Staff's Fate
Upon completion of the Quest for the Disks of Mishakal — specifically, if a PC uses the staff to strike Khisanth in area 70k — the staff **shatters on contact**. Blue light bursts from it in brilliant spherical waves. The person who broke the staff disappears in light. The blue wave shakes the earth, covers the dragon, and the dragon falls screaming into light. The cavern begins to collapse.

The person who broke the staff reappears at the statue of Mishakal (area 46b), unharmed with all HP restored. The staff becomes part of the statue. The PC who broke it now wears a **platinum Medallion of Faith** bearing the symbol of the goddess.

## THE DISKS OF MISHAKAL

The primary objective of the adventure. Located in Khisanth's hoard at area 70k.

### Physical Description
- Platinum disks, 18 inches in diameter
- Each disk 1/16 inch thick
- **160 plates in total**
- A bolt passes through one side of the plates, allowing each to swivel out and be viewed while keeping the stack together
- Each plate is engraved on both sides

### Alignment Restriction
- **LG or NG alignment:** May examine the plates freely
- **Other alignments:** Take **4d6 points of electrical damage** each time they try to touch or read the disks

### What the Disks Contain
Engraved knowledge about the ancient Gods of Good:
| God | Domain |
|-----|--------|
| **Paladine** | Rulership |
| **Majere** | Meditation and control |
| **Kiri-Jolith** | War and battle |
| **Mishakal** | Healing |
| **Habakuk** | Seas and animals |
| **Branchala** | Elves, forests, and music |

### Effect
Any cleric who reads this book and worships one of these gods may receive spells per **standard AD&D rules**. The book tells how to worship them and gain true clerical abilities. This **restores clerical spellcasting to Krynn**.

## MEDALLION OF FAITH

### Creation
The platinum Medallion of Faith is created when the Crystal Staff is shattered against Khisanth. It appears around the neck of the PC who broke the staff.

The medallion is the symbol of those who worship the true gods. Each medallion **magically creates another medallion of faith** for clerics of any good alignment who profess faith in the old gods of good. The created medallion bears the symbol of the god that the cleric professes, regardless of which symbol the original bears.

### Powers
- No powers beyond creation of additional medallions as described above
- Serves as a symbol of faith
- True believers wear them at all times, although never openly

## CURRENCY QUICK REFERENCE (see DL1_01 for full details)

All treasures found in Xak Tsaroth are in **Tsarothian clay Culli** (completely worthless in the present age) or in metal items with intrinsic value. The 30,000 Culli in area 70d are worthless except as the tax payment for the Clerk Spectral Minion in area 57a.

**Steel pieces = XP:** 1 steel = 1 XP for experience purposes.

**Gems and metals** have gpv/gpw values as noted in the standard AD&D exchange (1 gpv = 1 gp equivalent for experience and purchasing purposes, converted at the rate of 1 steel Emas = 50 sp in Seeker Lands).
